What is a language?

Its a question that sounds simple.

But can it be easy to answer such question if doing so requires using it itself?

Perhaps to understand what a language is one should have a limitation of knowing only one.

The task would be semingly impossible for someone who knows more than one language.

So perhaps in this case the question would have to be more specific to include what language the question is refering to?

An assumption could be made that if english is being used for this writing then it would also be the subject language requiring a definition.

Languages?

I happen to know more than a few so my definitions might get a bit complicated as sometimes the process of thinking in one gets expressed in another and the imterpretation is being done seemingly unnoticably and I end up writing something in one language that perhaps should have been done in another.

Lithuanian

I live in a country that has a history of its language being represed.

The people that did not obey the rules and traveled thru forests bringing books in Lithuanian are now considered heroes.

Yet at the time not everyone was either willing to do so or help those that did.

It is because of their effort the not only Lithuanian language but also the country exists.

The years the country remembers as oppresion are the years it was a part of Soviet Union.

Perhaps not every aspect of Lithuanian language was banned because there are a lot of literature that was printed in Lithuanian during that time.

So perhaps the question was that of the content that was printed inside the books.

Yes. Parts of it was filled with communist propaganda and other dictatorial non-sense.

But there were also physics books and those of math that had all the content that was need printed in a single book.

One book for all school years was enough. And that book was available for free.
